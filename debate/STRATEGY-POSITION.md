## Claim 1 — Novelty
The thesis is **NOT novel** and is in fact saturated in 2026 (Golden Krishna's
*The Best Interface Is No Interface*, 2015; Mark Weiser's calm computing, 1990s;
and in 2026: Nothing's Carl Pei, *The Week*'s "Agentic Pivot," a wall of Medium
"death of the app" posts). Leading with the thesis makes PJ essay #4,001.

The **defensible edge** is the triad: (a) an instrumented n=1 self-experiment
with a hard number (78%), (b) a *mechanism* — memory-at-volume without noise
collapse + reliable/replicable/**verifiable** agents (Cortex/Mitosis), and
(c) a contrarian position the failures validate: *the unlock is reliability/
verification, not new hardware.*

## Claim 2 — "Viral" is the wrong KPI for 2026
LinkedIn now suppresses pure virality and rewards niche authority; the
respect-and-adoption goal is better served by **visibility to the right ~500
people** (CTOs, founders, investors, press) than by raw impressions. Chase
velocity on **X**, authority on **LinkedIn**. Don't optimize the like counter.

## Claim 3 — Route: a sequenced pyramid, not one channel
1. **Anchor (control + respect):** an owned **technical white paper / arXiv
   preprint** on the mechanism. NOT a peer-reviewed venue first (too slow, n=1
   won't pass). This is what separates PJ from the Medium horde.
2. **Vector (reach):** the rewritten first-person essay — X thread + LinkedIn
   long-form — proof-first, contrarian, linking to the white paper.
3. **Amplify (prestige):** a *real editorial* op-ed (Forbes/Fast Company/The
   Information/Wired) pitched **after** traction. **NOT** a paid "Forbes
   Council" slot — that's respect-negative.
4. **Capture (adoption):** a Mitosis "Build your own Adam" page + email capture.
   Without this, the campaign is vanity, not growth.

## Claim 4 — Two things Grok's earlier plan missed (the most important)
- **Skeptic defense must be baked into every asset.** Two objections *will* be
  used: (i) "an agent just opens apps for you — that's not the death of the app";
  (ii) "this is Humane/Rabbit again" ($240M hardware graveyard; post-mortem line:
  *"AI doesn't need a new gadget; it needs to improve the tools you already
  use"*). PJ's essay's Bee-band + hacked-Google-Home details walk into (ii) —
  reframe them as "software substrate, not gadget."
- **Destination before broadcast.** The capture page + white paper must be live
  *before* the essay posts, or the attention spike is wasted and skeptics frame
  the idea during the exact window PJ has attention.

## Claim 5 — Sequencing (the one real disagreement with Grok's earlier plan)
Grok said "X thread first." I say: **capture page first; white paper + essay
near-simultaneous.** Velocity matters but PJ's goal is adoption + control, not a
spike someone else interprets.
